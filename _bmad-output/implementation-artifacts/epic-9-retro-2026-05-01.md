## Patterns from deep story reads

### What worked well

- **Single catalog seam repeatedly honored:** Stories point to `getDefaultCatalogAdapter`, `quoteCartLines`, and static/seed parity (FR-CAT-001)—reduces forked merchandising logic.
- **Defense in depth for `coming_soon`:** Listable in UI **and** rejected at quote/payment—even if seed drifts, checkout stays honest (`9-3`).
- **Explicit sequencing in sprint comments:** 9-4 hard-gating on 9-3 and Navbar/search coordination (`9-2`/`9-5`) reduced merge conflicts reflected in recorded completion notes.
- **Review checkpoints:** Multiple stories captured resolved vs deferred review items explicitly (e.g. PLP pricing for coming soon, privacy retention wording patch in `9-5`).

### Friction and risks (systems, not individuals)

- **Catalog/test coupling:** SKU renames (`9-1`) and assortment growth forced broad test greps (`handlers`, PDP, smoke). Pattern: centralized “fixture SKU” helpers or catalogs could reduce shotgun updates (optional improvement—not blocking).
- **JSON ↔ seed drift:** `9-4` explicitly fixed category mismatches (`boxer-briefs`). Treat parity checks as a recurring checklist whenever either file changes.
- **Tooling noise accepted:** `9-3` review notes browserslist staleness and jsdom/router warnings—not failures, but they add interpreter load in CI logs.
- **Smoke brittleness:** `9-5` flagged smoke coupling to multi-tile `/products` (bundle breadth vs copy story scope)—document as conscious trade-off; revisit if catalog narrows again.
- **Print edge:** `9-6` deferred “print while loading”—screen guard could be a small follow-up if support tickets appear.

3. **Operational polish:** Fixed assortment and print views move the product from demo to ops-usable workflows.
